Date : March 10

Event Description
SynCode is a tag team coding competition with each team consisting of exactly two members where each member has to code the solutions to the questions provided in their systems that are placed away from each other. A buzzer will ring at particular intervals of time upon which they will have to swap the systems with their team mate. The event will have a single round consisting of four questions of medium difficulty, each carrying equal points. If deemed necessary, there will be a preliminary round held prior to the event.

Rules and regulations
There will be a swapping of systems within the members of the team at every successive 30, 20, 20, 10 and 10 minutes respectively.
The participants are not allowed to use the internet or any other external sources during the event.
College IDs are compulsory.
The programming languages that can be used are C, C++, Java and Python but both members of a team must choose the same language for coding.
Members of a team needn’t be from the same institution.
A participation certificate will be issued for all the participants of the event.
The decisions of the judge and the coordinators regarding the events are full and final. No arguments will be tolerated regarding the same.
Any type of communication (including comments in the program code) between the team members are strictly prohibited during the event. The violation of above will lead to the immediate disqualification of the team.
